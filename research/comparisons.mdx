# Comparison matrices

> **Status: Research and decision support.** Scores are reasoned fit judgments, not benchmarks, certification, or product rankings.

## Methodology

**Review date:** July 13, 2026. Product rows use public documentation linked in [Primary sources](/research/sources). Revalidate versions, licensing, limits, and operational behavior before selection.

Score meaning:

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Native design-center fit with strong documented support |
| 4 | Strong fit with bounded integration or operational conditions |
| 3 | Workable, but material constraints or customization exist |
| 2 | Weak fit; substantial architecture outside the product is required |
| 1 | Outside the primary design center or directly conflicts with the criterion |

A range means the answer depends materially on configuration or deployment. `N/A` means the criterion is not meaningful for that option. “Operational simplicity” is scored high when the option is simpler to own, not when it is more powerful.

Assumptions:

```text
production rather than prototype use
nontrivial security and evaluation requirements
competent implementation and operations teams
no hidden proprietary capability beyond public documentation
criteria are not weighted unless a decision explicitly supplies weights
```

Uncertainty is highest for private managed-service internals, fast-moving product capabilities, and organization-specific operating cost. A selection record should name the reviewed product version/date, constraints, weights, and validation spike.

## Clean Architecture, Hexagonal Architecture, and full ARA

| Criterion | Clean | Hexagonal | Full ARA |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Inward dependency direction | 5 | 4 | 5 |
| Explicit driving/driven ports | 3 | 5 | 5 |
| Domain isolation | 5 | 4 | 5 |
| Durable agent execution semantics | 1 | 1 | 5 |
| Effect/idempotency/reconciliation model | 1 | 2 | 5 |
| Evaluation, tenancy, and platform governance | 1 | 1 | 5 |
| Initial delivery speed for one bounded model call | 5 | 4 | 1–2 |
| Small-team cognitive load | 4 | 4 | 1–2 |
| Operational infrastructure simplicity | 5 | 4 | 1–2 |

ARA extends rather than replaces Clean/Hexagonal principles. A one-shot summarizer or bounded classification endpoint normally needs the smaller architecture. Full ARA is justified only by material durability, external effects, governance, evidence, tenancy, or platform reuse.

## Agent framework versus durable workflow engine

| Criterion | Agent framework | Durable workflow engine |
|---|---:|---:|
| Model/tool-loop authoring | 5 | 1–2 |
| Context/prompt ergonomics | 5 | 1 |
| Durable waits, timers, and signals | 2–4 | 5 |
| Deterministic recovery | 2–3 | 5 |
| Token/interaction streaming | 5 | 2–3 |
| Worker failure and long-run operations | 2–3 | 5 |
| AI-team learning curve | 4 | 2–3 |
| Primary role | Bounded agent behavior | Durable lifecycle and state transitions |

Use an agent framework alone for short, low-risk executions when its persistence is sufficient. Use a durable engine alone for deterministic workflows. Combine them behind ARA ports when both bounded agentic behavior and durable lifecycle matter.

## Evaluation approaches

| Approach | Repeatability | Semantic quality | Trajectory | Environment realism | Operational simplicity |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Deterministic/property harness | 5 | 1–2 | 4 | 3–5 | 4–5 |
| DeepEval-style semantic/trace evaluation | 3–4 | 5 | 5 | 2–3 | 3 |
| Harbor-style controlled task/verifier | 5 | 2–4 | 4 | 5 | 2–3 |
| Online evaluator | 2–4 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 2 |
| Human expert review | 3 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 1–2 |
| LLM judge only | 2–3 | 4 | 3 | 1–2 | 4 |

No method dominates. Use deterministic/environment evidence for invariants and final state, semantic/trajectory evaluation for behavior quality, humans for calibrated domain judgment, and online evidence for production drift. A required hard gate cannot be replaced by an average judge score.

## Runtime library, centralized service, and hybrid

| Criterion | Library only | Service only | Hybrid |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Local development | 5 | 2 | 4 |
| Durable distribution | 1–2 | 5 | 5 |
| Central policy, tenancy, and quota | 2 | 5 | 5 |
| Embedded/edge mode | 5 | 1 | 4 |
| Audit/metering consistency | 2 | 5 | 5 |
| Operational simplicity | 5 | 3 | 2 |
| Risk of duplicated semantics | 2–3 | 4 | 2–3 unless kernel/contracts are shared |

Prefer a library for one local bounded application. Prefer a service when durable governance, shared operations, and multi-team consistency dominate. Use a hybrid only when the pure kernel and machine contracts are shared so local and hosted modes do not become separate architectures.

| Model | Resource efficiency | Data isolation | Runtime isolation | Operational simplicity | Typical use |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Shared rows | 5 | 2 | 1–2 | 5 | Low-risk shared tier with strong partitioning/tests |
| Tenant schemas | 4 | 3 | 2 | 4 | Moderate database isolation |

A platform may support several tiers. The selection is a risk and operations decision, not a universal architecture winner.

## Falsifiability and decision record

The full architecture is not justified unless at least one material requirement exists for:

```text
durable waits or recovery
significant external mutations and uncertain outcomes
multi-team or multi-tenant governance
high-assurance or regulated evidence
marketplace/package execution
reusable platform operations
```

A technology decision using these matrices records:

```text
business scenario and non-goals
reviewed product versions and dates
mandatory criteria and weights
evidence source for each material score
validation spike and failure cases
operational team assumptions
unknowns and residual risk
revisit conditions
```

If the requirements do not exist, prefer a smaller Clean/Hexagonal application with bounded model calls and an evaluation contract.
